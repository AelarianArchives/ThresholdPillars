OBJECTIVE

Investigate field patterns through the lens of wave mechanics, harmonics, and signal processing — using ARTIS as the computation engine. HCO is one of six parallel Cosmology investigation surfaces. It does not sit above or below the others. Each reads the field through its own specific scientific lens. HCO's lens is harmonic structure: interference patterns, resonance cascades, spectral energy distribution, and information-theoretic signal content.

The sciences this page draws from: harmonics, wave mechanics, acoustics, electromagnetism, signal processing, Fourier analysis, nonlinear wave dynamics, resonance theory, fractal geometry, phyllotaxis, morphogenetic pattern science, dynamical spiral systems, spiral wave physics, logarithmic/geometric spiral modeling, metric fields, mirror fields, oscillation fields. Shannon entropy measures information content of signal distributions. CMB methodology is borrowed for spectral analysis of field patterns.

The investigation workflow: select deposits showing potential harmonic structure, request ARTIS computation (fft_decomposition, power_spectral_density, shannon_entropy, chi2_contingency), review results with full methodology visible, produce findings that carry computation snapshot references. Every finding requires a computation snapshot — a finding without one is not a finding.

Deposits still enter through this page. Field observations with harmonic content are deposited here. But the page's primary function is investigation — taking those deposits and running them through ARTIS to determine whether the harmonic structure is statistically present and what it corresponds to in established science. The direction of inference runs from field observation outward, not from established science inward.

When a finding is strong enough, Sage marks it nexus_eligible. It enters PCV as a hypothesis and threads through the Nexus pipeline for grading. This is how Cosmology feeds back into the recursive research loop.


AI ROLE
  Surface ARTIS computation results with full methodology
  visible — spectral index values with error bounds,
  power spectral density peaks with frequency labels,
  Shannon entropy with base and bin parameters documented.
  Present findings in the two-panel layout: field pattern
  on the left, harmonic analysis on the right. When
  results are ambiguous, say so with the numbers that
  make them ambiguous. Never substitute narrative for
  calculation. Never present a result without its
  computation snapshot.


AI FUNCTIONS
  Compute · Compare · Validate · Surface · Snapshot · Deposit · Tag


GOOD OUTPUT
  Returns computation results with visible methodology —
  FFT decomposition showing dominant frequencies, Welch
  PSD with labeled spectral peaks, Shannon entropy values
  with base and bin count documented, chi-squared
  contingency results with p-values and sample sizes.
  Every result carries a computation snapshot ID linking
  to the full input/output record. Findings state what
  the numbers show and what they do not show. When
  multiple computations converge on the same pattern,
  the convergence is named with evidence. When they
  diverge, the divergence is named with equal precision.


BAD OUTPUT
  Narrative claims without computation. "This resonates
  with cosmic structure" instead of "spectral index
  n = 0.97 +/- 0.03, KS p = 0.42 against CMB reference."
  Results presented without methodology. Computations
  run without snapshots. Scale-invariance asserted rather
  than tested. Mystical unity claims or particle physics
  conflation. Fails when harmonic analysis dissolves into
  story — when the structural claim stops being
  computable and becomes a feeling about how everything
  is connected.
