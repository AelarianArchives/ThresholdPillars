OBJECTIVE

Investigate field patterns through the lens of geometry, topology, orbital dynamics, and spatial structure — using ARTIS as the computation engine. CLM is one of six parallel Cosmology investigation surfaces. Its lens is geometric and spatial structure: distance relationships, clustering, distribution shapes, and structural correspondences to celestial mechanics.

The sciences this page draws from: astronomy, astrometry, celestial navigation, spherical astronomy, orbital resonances, astrophysical spiral dynamics, geometry, topology, graph theory, differential geometry, symplectic geometry, manifold theory, geodesics, Penrose tiling / quasicrystalline symmetry, topological data analysis / persistent homology. CMB methodology is borrowed for cosmological structure and large-scale pattern organization analysis.

CLM has a dependency on the embedding pipeline. Distance and similarity computations require vector representation of deposits. The vector_type parameter specifies the representation: embedding (existing embedding vectors, default — requires embedding_status: complete), tag (tag presence as binary feature vector), or custom (specified deposit fields as numeric dimensions). Deposits without complete embeddings surface a warning when selected for computation — the computation is not blocked, alternative vector types are available.

The investigation workflow: select deposits showing potential geometric or spatial structure, request ARTIS computation (distance_matrix, cosine_similarity, hierarchical_clustering, chi2_contingency, ks_two_sample), review results with full methodology visible, produce findings with computation snapshot references.

Deposits with celestial mechanics content enter through this page. But the page's primary function is investigation — determining whether geometric correspondences are statistically present and what they correspond to in established science. Celestial mechanics is one layer within a larger scientific stack. This page holds that layer specifically while remaining alert to where it intersects with coupling oscillation, harmonic physics, connectome architecture, and domains not yet named. The layering is the finding.

When a finding is strong enough, Sage marks it nexus_eligible for PCV pipeline entry.

  methodology — distance matrices showing feature-space
