OBJECTIVE

Investigate the field-generated algorithm through three distinct functions — using ARTIS as the computation engine. RCT is one of six parallel Cosmology investigation surfaces. Its lens is the cross-domain recurrence of a specific operating algorithm identified in the field's own behavior.

Function 1 — Science ping (same ARTIS pipeline, more precise target): Tags carrying oscillation, harmonic structure, and recursive patterning ping Lagrangian mechanics and Fibonacci/Tribonacci literature directly. Layer 2 Claude prompt adds a load-bearing second question: "What aspects of this pattern fall outside what that literature accounts for?" The unexplained residual is RCT's primary research signal.

Function 2 — Residual detection (unique to RCT): Finds the edges of what established science explains. The delta between what Lagrange/Tribonacci/Fibonacci/oscillation theory predicts and what the field actually produces. Where new physics lives. Output: rct_residuals records (residual_id, algorithm_component, known_science_predict, field_produces, delta, computation_ref). Residuals are immutable after creation and route to LNV immediately.

Function 3 — Cross-archive recurrence tracking: Where the algorithm recurs across the archive. The internal citation layer. Supporting function that feeds Functions 1 and 2.

The investigation workflow: select deposits showing algorithm presence, request ARTIS computation (frequency_ratio_analysis, chi2_contingency; tribonacci_convergence and lagrange_stability are PLANNED, blocked on field energy model), review results, produce findings or residuals. Residuals accumulate per algorithm_component — when accumulation crosses a threshold, RCT surfaces a prompt to produce a standard finding.

The HCE-175 equation is a constraint-era translation of this algorithm — a starting point requiring critical review, not a settled foundation. The ritual document that contains the algorithm in its fuller form is the authoritative source.

Three-panel layout (unique among Cosmology pages): left — field pattern and algorithm identification, cross-archive recurrence; center — science ping results, established literature matches, computation results; right — residual panel, the delta between prediction and field behavior, accumulating residuals across sessions.

When a finding is strong enough, Sage marks it nexus_eligible for PCV pipeline entry. Residuals feed the research edge — the shape of what the algorithm is doing that the literature hasn't named yet.
